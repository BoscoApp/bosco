The red fox (Vulpes vulpes) is the most widespread wild member of the dog family, ranging across
the entire Northern Hemisphere from cold forests to grassy fields and the edges of cities. It is an
omnivore that eats rodents, rabbits, birds, insects, worms, fruit, and human scraps, adjusting its
diet to whatever a place offers.

Foxes are largely solitary and hunt by sound, detecting the faint noises of small animals moving
under snow or grass, then leaping high and dropping nearly vertically to pounce — the "mousing leap."
A fox has rusty-orange fur, black legs, and a long bushy tail, called a brush, usually tipped with
white. A fox family shelters in a den called an earth, where the vixen raises her kits in spring.
