The red fox (_Vulpes vulpes_) is the largest of the true foxes and the most widely distributed
carnivore on Earth, ranging across the entire Northern Hemisphere and introduced to Australia. Its
success comes from remarkable adaptability: it is an omnivore that will eat rodents, rabbits,
birds, insects, worms, fruit, and human scraps, adjusting its diet to whatever a place offers.

Foxes are largely solitary and do not hunt in packs as wolves do. They rely on acute hearing to
locate prey, and can detect the low-frequency sounds of small animals moving underground. When
hunting in snow or tall grass, a fox performs a high, arching pounce — the **mousing leap** —
dropping nearly vertically onto its target. A fox family shelters in a den called an **earth**,
where the female (a vixen) raises her kits through the spring.
