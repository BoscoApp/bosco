## St. John Bosco (1815–1888)

Giovanni Melchiorre Bosco was born on 16 August 1815 at Becchi, a hamlet near Turin in the Kingdom
of Piedmont-Sardinia. His father died when John was two, leaving his mother, Margaret, to raise the
family in poverty. A famous dream at the age of nine — in which he saw himself among unruly boys who
were tamed not by blows but by "gentleness and charity" — set the direction of his whole life.

### The Oratory

Ordained a priest in 1841, Don Bosco began gathering the neglected apprentice boys of rapidly
industrializing Turin. What started as a Sunday catechism grew into the **Oratory of St. Francis de
Sales**: a stable home offering lodging, schooling, workshops (shoemaking, tailoring, and printing
among them), and religious formation. He deliberately united _play and catechesis_ — recreation,
music, and theater alongside the sacraments — a synthesis that became the model for his schools.

### The Preventive System

Don Bosco articulated an educational method he called the **Preventive System**, resting on three
pillars: _reason, religion, and loving-kindness_ (_ragione, religione, amorevolezza_). Rather than
repress wrongdoing after the fact, the educator surrounds the young with such active goodness —
constant, affectionate presence — that occasions of sin are prevented. "It is not enough that the
young should be loved," he wrote; "they must know that they are loved."

### The Salesians

To carry the work forward he founded the **Society of St. Francis de Sales (the Salesians)** in
1859, and, with St. Mary Mazzarello, the Daughters of Mary Help of Christians for the education of
girls. By his death on 31 January 1888 the Salesians numbered in the hundreds and had spread beyond
Italy; today they are among the largest religious congregations in the Church. He was canonized in 1934. His feast is kept on 31 January.
