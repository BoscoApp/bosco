## The boy who juggled for God

John Bosco was born in 1815 on a small farm in northern Italy. His father died when John was very
young, and the family was poor. As a boy, John taught himself acrobatics, juggling, and magic
tricks. He would gather the village children to watch his show — but there was a rule: first they
prayed the Rosary and listened as he repeated the priest's Sunday sermon, and _then_ came the tricks.

## Don Bosco and the Oratory

John became a priest and was known ever after as "Don Bosco" (_Don_ is Italian for a priest). In the
city of Turin he found many poor and rough boys with no one to guide them. He gathered them into a
place he called the **Oratory** — a home where they could play games, learn a trade like shoemaking
or printing, go to school, and grow close to God.

## The kind way

Don Bosco believed in what he called the "preventive system." Instead of punishing boys after they
did wrong, he kept them so busy with good and happy things that they had little chance to go wrong.
"It is not enough to love the young," he said; "they must know that they are loved." He founded the
Salesians, an order of priests and brothers who still teach children all over the world today.

Don Bosco died in 1888. His feast is kept on January 31.
