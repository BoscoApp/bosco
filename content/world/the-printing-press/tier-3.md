## The printing press

The movable-type printing press developed by Johannes Gutenberg of Mainz around 1440 is often
called one of the most consequential inventions in human history. Gutenberg combined several
existing ideas — the screw press, oil-based ink, and above all reusable cast-metal type — into a
system that made the mass production of texts practical for the first time in Europe.

His masterpiece, the **Gutenberg Bible** (c. 1455), demonstrated that printed books could rival the
finest manuscripts in beauty while being produced in far greater numbers. Within fifty years,
presses had spread to hundreds of European cities, and millions of books were in circulation. Rising
literacy, the standardization of texts, and the rapid spread of ideas all followed.
