## Books before printing

For most of history, every book had to be copied out by hand, letter by letter, usually by monks
in monasteries. A single Bible could take a scribe years to finish. Books were so rare and precious
that they were sometimes chained to desks.

## Gutenberg's invention

Around the year 1440, a German craftsman named **Johannes Gutenberg** invented a printing press that
used movable metal type — small metal letters that could be arranged, inked, pressed onto paper, and
then rearranged to print the next page. Suddenly a workshop could print hundreds of copies of a book
in the time it once took to copy one by hand.
