## A clever hunter

The red fox is a member of the dog family, but it behaves a little like a cat. It has a slender
body, a pointed face, and a long, bushy tail called a "brush." Its coat is usually reddish-orange,
with black legs and a white tip on the tail.

Foxes have wonderful ears. They can hear a mouse squeaking under the snow. When a fox hears its
prey, it stands very still, then leaps high into the air and comes down with its front paws to pin
the mouse. This funny hop is called "mousing."

## Fox families

In spring, a mother fox (called a vixen) has a litter of pups, or "kits," in an underground den.
Both parents help feed them. By autumn the young foxes are grown and go off to find dens of their
own.

Foxes eat almost anything: mice, rabbits, insects, berries, and eggs. Being willing to eat many
foods is one reason foxes live almost everywhere — in forests, fields, and even near towns.
